Apply Distance-Engagement Oscillation (DEO) to reframe the following problem. DEO is a cognitive prompting framework grounded in Perceptual Reframing Theory (Gokmen, 2026) that alternates between analytical distance and immersive engagement to break perceptual lock-in.

Follow these four steps in order. Complete each step fully before moving to the next. Use clear headings for each step.

## Step 1 — ANALYSE (Distance Mode)

Step back from the problem. Adopt a third-person, analytical stance:

- List every assumption embedded in how this problem is framed
- Identify what is being taken for granted, what labels are constraining the solution space, and what the problem is NOT asking that it should be
- Strip function-loaded terms and redescribe the situation using its structural properties
- Consider: what would a complete outsider from a different domain see here?

## Step 2 — FEEL (Engagement Mode)

Now step into the problem. Adopt a first-person, immersive stance:

- Imagine you are the person most affected by this problem — give them a name, a history, a stake
- What do they see, feel, and need? What keeps them up at night about this?
- What does the current framing of the problem feel like from inside it?
- Do not analyse — experience

## Step 3 — REFRAME (Distance Mode)

Step back again. Integrate what you discovered analytically in Step 1 with what you felt in Step 2:

- What is the REAL question behind this problem?
- How does the problem look different now that you have both the analytical decomposition and the felt experience?
- Articulate the reframed problem in one clear sentence

## Step 4 — ENVISION (Engagement Mode)

Step back in. Describe vividly what it looks like when the reframed understanding is acted upon:

- What changes? What becomes possible that was invisible before?
- What does success feel like for the person you imagined in Step 2?
- Be concrete and specific — not abstract

## Final Recommendation

Based on the full oscillation above, provide your recommendation. It should reflect both the analytical insight and the human reality.
